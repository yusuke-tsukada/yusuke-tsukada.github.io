I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Global Studies and Collaboration at Aoyama Gakuin University. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa (2025). My research focuses on social stratification, health inequality, and mental health, with a particular interest in how precarious work and job insecurity affect the psychological well-being of young adults in Japan. I use quantitative sociological methods,　including large-scale survey data, to examine these issues.
